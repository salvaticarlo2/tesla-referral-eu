Spanish EV Grants: MOVES III

Spain's flagship electric vehicle subsidy programme, MOVES III, offers up to €9,000 off the purchase price of an eligible electric vehicle — and Teslas typically qualify.

How much can you get?

Eligibility requirements

  • Vehicle must be new (not used) and registered in Spain
  • Purchase price must not exceed €45,000 (before VAT) — Model 3 SR qualifies; higher trims may not
  • The grant is applied at point of sale (Tesla dealerships handle this)
  • MOVES III funds are managed by regional governments; availability varies by Comunidad Autónoma

> Important: MOVES III funds are finite and allocated per region. Apply as early as possible — funds in high-demand regions like Madrid and Catalonia can run out mid-year.

Business & Autónomo Tax Benefits

Buying a Tesla through a Spanish business or as an autónomo unlocks significant tax advantages:

IVA deduction (VAT)

  • Self-employed professionals and companies can deduct 100% of the 21% IVA if the vehicle is used exclusively for business
  • Mixed use (personal + business): typically 50% deductible — consult your gestor for your specific situation

Amortisation

  • Electric vehicles qualify for accelerated amortisation under Spanish tax law
  • A Tesla Model 3 or Y can be written off over 5–8 years depending on the regime

Electricity costs

  • Charging costs at home or at the office are tax-deductible as business expenses
  • Install a Wallbox charger at your business premises — the installation cost is also deductible

Reduced impuesto de matriculación

  • Spain's vehicle registration tax (impuesto especial sobre determinados medios de transporte) is €0 for zero-emission vehicles — a saving of up to €2,000–5,000 on petrol equivalents

Tesla Supercharger Network in Spain (2026)

Tesla's Spanish Supercharger network has expanded significantly, making long-distance travel straightforward even from Spain's more remote regions.

Coverage highlights

  • Major corridors covered: Madrid–Barcelona (A-2), Madrid–Seville (A-4), Madrid–Valencia (A-3), Barcelona–French border (AP-7), Madrid–Portugal (A-5/A-6)
  • Over 80 Supercharger stations across the country as of early 2026
  • Key hubs: Madrid (5+ stations), Barcelona (4+ stations), Valencia, Seville, Bilbao, Malaga, Zaragoza
  • Island coverage: Mallorca, Gran Canaria, Tenerife — all have Supercharger access
  • V3 Superchargers deployed at major motorway locations: up to 250 kW charging speed

Typical charging cost

  • Supercharger pricing in Spain: approximately €0.30–0.40 per kWh (varies by location and time)
  • A full charge from 10% to 80% for a Model Y Long Range (≈55 kWh) costs approximately €16–22
  • Tesla app shows real-time pricing before you arrive

Home charging

  • Spanish electricity tariffs (PVPC) range from €0.08–0.20/kWh depending on the time slot
  • Charging a Model 3 overnight (off-peak): approximately €5–8 for a full charge

Why Spain's Climate Makes Tesla Ownership Ideal

Spain's sunny, mild climate is practically designed for EV ownership:

  • Minimal range loss in winter — unlike Nordic countries, Spanish winters rarely affect battery performance significantly
  • Solar energy synergy — Spain has Europe's highest solar irradiance. If you have solar panels at home, you can charge your Tesla for near-zero cost during daylight hours
  • Long driving distances — Spain's vast geography (the second-largest country in the EU) is now fully covered by Tesla's Supercharger network for intercity travel
  • Year-round low maintenance — no diesel glow plugs, no engine oil, no clutch: ideal for Spain's stop-start urban traffic
