UK Government Incentives: What's Available in 2026

The UK's EV incentive landscape has changed significantly since 2023. Here's where things stand:

Consumer Plug-in Grant — ENDED

Unfortunately, the UK government's consumer Plug-in Car Grant (PiCG) ended in June 2022. Private buyers can no longer claim direct government subsidies on EV purchases.

Company Car Tax (Benefit in Kind)

This is where the UK still shines for business users. Electric vehicles continue to attract a 2% Benefit in Kind (BiK) rate — compared to 25-37% for petrol or diesel equivalents. For a higher-rate taxpayer running a Model Y Long Range as a company car, the annual tax savings over a combustion vehicle can exceed £5,000 per year.

This benefit is confirmed through the 2027-28 tax year, with gradual increases planned from 2025 onwards (reaching 5% by 2027-28). Still substantially lower than ICE vehicles.

Salary Sacrifice Schemes

Many UK employers now offer Tesla via salary sacrifice — you sacrifice gross salary to "pay" for the car, reducing income tax and National Insurance. For a 40% taxpayer, a Tesla Model Y Long Range can effectively cost £600-700/month gross before fuel savings. Schemes are available through providers like Octopus EV, Onto, and LeasePlan.

London ULEZ and Congestion Charge

Tesla drivers in London are exempt from the Congestion Charge (£15/day saving) and the Ultra Low Emission Zone (ULEZ) charge. For anyone commuting into central London regularly, this alone can represent over £3,000 in annual savings.

Scotland: Interest-Free EV Loans

Scottish residents can apply for the Energy Saving Trust's Low Carbon Transport Loan — up to £35,000 interest-free for new EVs. This effectively reduces the cost of a Tesla Model 3 or Y over the loan term by several thousand pounds.

Tesla Supercharger Network UK

The UK has one of Europe's most mature Supercharger networks — over 700 Supercharger stalls at 150+ locations as of early 2026.

Key coverage highlights:

  • M25 orbital corridor: Stalls every 30-40 miles, ideal for commuting or ring-road coverage
  • M1, M6, M4, A1: Major arteries fully covered with 150-250 kW V3 chargers
  • Scotland: Glasgow, Edinburgh, Aberdeen, Inverness all served — Highland drives now viable
  • Wales: Cardiff and Swansea covered; mid-Wales can require planning
  • Northern Ireland: Belfast Titanic Quarter + Lisburn road now open (2025 addition)

Average UK Supercharger session cost: £0.52–0.62/kWh (on-peak). For a Model Y Long Range, a 10-80% charge typically costs £12-18 — comparable to a full tank of petrol but vastly cheaper over time.

At home, most UK buyers install a home wallbox (7.4kW standard). Government OZEV grants of £350 are available via the EV chargepoint grant scheme for eligible homeowners.

Finance Options in the UK

Tesla UK offers:

  • PCP (Personal Contract Purchase): Typical deposit 15-20%, 24-48 month terms, balloon payment at end
  • Tesla Finance (HP): Straightforward hire purchase
  • Business Contract Hire (BCH): Most popular for company cars
  • Leasing via brokers: Sites like carwow, Lease Fetcher, and Vanarama often beat Tesla's direct lease prices

For business users, section 100% first-year allowance means you can write off the full cost of a new EV against taxable profits in year one — a significant tax advantage vs. a 25% pool allowance on ICE vehicles.

UK-Specific Considerations Before You Buy

Right-hand drive: UK Teslas are RHD — the driver's display and controls are mirrored accordingly. UK spec is fully localized.

Software updates: UK vehicles receive OTA updates aligned with European rollout schedules, including Full Self-Driving (FSD) Supervised, now available in the UK since late 2023 as a subscription (£99/month or ~£7,500 purchase).

Charging away from Superchargers: Tesla's CCS2 port (UK uses Type 2/CCS) is compatible with all major UK public networks — BP Pulse, Osprey, Pod Point, Gridserve Electric Highway, and Instavolt. With a CHAdeMO adapter, you can also use Chademo-equipped chargers.

Insurance group: Most Tesla models fall in insurance groups 40-50 — higher than ICE equivalents. Shop around; EV specialist insurers like Admiral, LV=, and Aviva EV are often competitive.
