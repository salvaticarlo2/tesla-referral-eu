What Is the Tesla Model Y L?

The Tesla Model Y L — also called the Model Y Long Wheelbase — is an extended-body version of Tesla's best-selling SUV. The "L" stands for Long: the vehicle is 177mm longer than the standard Model Y, giving it a proper third row of seating for 6 adults.

This is not the same as the existing 7-seat Model Y option available in Europe today. The current 7-seat variant squeezes two small seats into the existing body — fine for children, impractical for adults. The Model Y L is a fundamentally larger vehicle, purpose-built for families who genuinely need 6 seats.

Tesla first launched the Model Y L in China, where demand for larger family EVs is growing rapidly. As of early 2026, Korea and Australia/New Zealand have received regulatory clearance for H1 2026 deliveries. The EU has not yet been announced — but all signs point to it being next.

Specs and Dimensions

Based on the China-market and Korea/AUS-cleared specifications, here's what the Model Y L brings:

Dimensions

  • Length: ~4,999mm (177mm longer than standard)
  • Wheelbase: Extended — exact EU figure TBC
  • Height: Slightly higher roofline for rear headroom
  • Seats: 6 (standard) or 7 (optional)

Powertrain (estimated for EU)

  • Range: 681km (AUS confirmed, 19" wheels) — EU WLTP likely 600–650km
  • Drive: AWD (Dual Motor) — 378 kW / 590 Nm (AUS confirmed)
  • Charging: CCS2 + Tesla Supercharger V3/V4
  • 0–100 km/h: 5.0 sec (AUS confirmed)
  • V2L (Vehicle-to-Load): 3.3 kW discharge — first time on a Model Y

Note: AUS specs confirmed March 13, 2026. EU specs will be confirmed at official EU launch announcement.
