US vs Europe: FSD Price Comparison

In the United States, Tesla FSD Supervised costs $8,000 as a one-time purchase. That has been the stable US price since mid-2025, after Tesla reduced it from $12,000.

For Europe, Tesla has not announced an official continent-wide price. Here is what we know:

  • Netherlands (live): Early signals suggest €3,000–€8,000 depending on model and existing Autopilot package
  • Germany, France, Norway: No confirmed pricing yet, but Tesla's EU configurator references FSD as an available upgrade
  • Currency conversion baseline: $8,000 converts to roughly €7,400 — but Tesla adjusts pricing for local markets rather than doing a straight conversion

We expect the European FSD one-time price to land between €6,000 and €8,000 without Enhanced Autopilot, and €3,000–€5,000 for those upgrading from EAP.

One-Time Purchase vs Monthly Subscription

Tesla offers two ways to get FSD in the US, and the same model is expected for Europe:

One-time purchase

  • Pay once, own it for the life of the vehicle
  • US price: $8,000 · Expected EU price: €6,000–€8,000
  • Transfers with the car if you sell it (adds resale value)
  • Best for owners who plan to keep the car 3+ years

Monthly subscription

  • US price: $99/month with Enhanced Autopilot, or $199/month without
  • Expected EU price: €99–€199/month (unconfirmed)
  • Cancel any time — no commitment
  • Does not add resale value
  • Best for trying FSD before committing, or occasional city drivers
Break-even math: At $99/month, the US subscription takes roughly 80 months (~6.5 years) to exceed the $8,000 one-time purchase. At $199/month without EAP, break-even is just 40 months (~3.3 years). European break-even will depend on final pricing, but the same logic applies: long-term owners save with the purchase, short-term owners save with the subscription.

Enhanced Autopilot vs FSD — What You Get for the Money

Before committing to FSD, it is worth understanding what Enhanced Autopilot (EAP) already gives you — and what FSD adds on top. For a detailed feature-by-feature breakdown, see our full Autopilot vs FSD comparison guide.

The short version:

  • Basic Autopilot (included free): Traffic-aware cruise control + lane centering on motorways
  • Enhanced Autopilot (~€3,800): Adds Navigate on Autopilot (motorway lane changes + on/off ramps), Autopark, Summon, and Smart Summon
  • FSD Supervised (€6,000–€8,000 or subscription): Everything in EAP plus city street driving — traffic lights, stop signs, roundabouts, unprotected turns, cyclist awareness

The gap between EAP and FSD is city street navigation. If you mostly drive on motorways and use Autopilot for highway cruising, EAP covers that. FSD matters when you want your Tesla to handle the full route — from your driveway through city streets to the motorway and back.

Should You Buy Now or Wait?

This depends on your situation. Here is a simple framework:

Buy FSD now (one-time purchase) if:

  • You plan to keep your Tesla for 3+ years and drive frequently in cities
  • You want to lock in the current price before Tesla adjusts European pricing
  • You care about resale value — FSD transfers with the vehicle

Wait for the subscription if:

  • You want to try FSD before committing thousands of euros
  • You mostly drive on motorways where Enhanced Autopilot is sufficient
  • You lease your Tesla and do not benefit from long-term ownership savings

Stick with Enhanced Autopilot if:

  • Your commute is almost entirely motorway driving
  • Navigate on Autopilot, Autopark, and Summon already cover your needs

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Tesla FSD cost in Europe?

Tesla FSD Supervised is expected to cost between €6,000 and €8,000 as a one-time purchase in Europe, or €3,000–€5,000 if upgrading from Enhanced Autopilot. Final pricing has not been officially announced by Tesla for all European markets.

Can I subscribe to Tesla FSD monthly in Europe?

Not yet, but it is expected. Tesla offers FSD as a $99–$199/month subscription in the US. Tesla's EU configurator references a subscription option, and a similar model is anticipated once FSD achieves full type approval in Europe after the offer ends0, 2026.

Is Enhanced Autopilot enough, or should I get FSD?

Enhanced Autopilot handles motorway driving, automatic lane changes, Autopark, and Summon. FSD adds city street navigation — traffic lights, stop signs, roundabouts, and unprotected turns. If most of your driving is on motorways, EAP is likely enough. For daily city driving, FSD is a meaningful upgrade. See our full Autopilot vs FSD comparison.

Is Tesla FSD cheaper in Europe than in the US?

We expect European FSD pricing to be similar to the US. In the US, FSD costs $8,000 one-time. Early European signals suggest €6,000–€8,000 for the full purchase. Tesla typically adjusts pricing for local markets rather than doing a straight currency conversion.

Should I buy FSD now or wait for the subscription?

If you plan to keep your Tesla 3+ years and drive regularly in cities, the one-time purchase offers better long-term value. If you want to try FSD first, drive mostly on motorways, or lease your car, the subscription is the smarter choice. The US subscription break-even vs purchase is roughly 5–7 years.
