Elon Musk confirmed at the November 2025 shareholder meeting: the Tesla Roadster production version reveals on April 1, 2026. After nearly a decade of delays, the fastest Tesla ever is finally coming.

The Tesla Roadster has been the most anticipated — and most delayed — car in Tesla's lineup. April 1, 2026 — What Tesla Will Reveal

At Tesla's November 2025 shareholder meeting, Elon Musk stated the Roadster production design will be revealed on April 1, 2026. Musk called it "the most unforgettable demo" — suggesting a live performance reveal rather than just a static design unveil.

What we expect Tesla to announce on April 1:

  • Final production design — Monoblock composite seats, radical aero body
  • Confirmed specs — Range, horsepower, 0–60 time
  • Pricing — Base price, SpaceX Package pricing (if still planned)
  • Delivery timeline — When reservation holders can expect invitations
  • New order window — Whether new reservations open post-reveal
Note on the date: April 1 is April Fool's Day. Tesla has historically used this date for genuine announcements (Model Y performance specs in 2021). The November 2025 shareholder meeting confirmation makes this real.

A new patent published in early 2026 showed a radical monoblock composite seat design — a structural seat integrated into the chassis rather than bolted on top. This dramatically reduces weight and signals Tesla is serious about record-breaking performance engineering. In March 2026, two new Roadster trademarks were filed — a strong signal that the production design is finalized and the April 1 event will debut the real production car, not just another prototype.

The SpaceX Package — originally teased as using cold-gas thrusters from SpaceX rocket technology — has not been officially confirmed or denied for the production version. Tesla executives have remained cryptic. April 1 should clarify this definitively.

EU Availability & Delivery Timeline

The Roadster is manufactured at Giga Texas (Austin). EU deliveries will ship from the US to Europe — likely via Rotterdam or Hamburg ports, similar to how Tesla ships Model 3 and Y from Giga Shanghai to Europe.

Expected timeline for Europe:

1
April 1, 2026 — Reveal
Production design confirmed. Tesla confirms delivery timeline and opens (or doesn't open) new reservations.
2
Q3–Q4 2026 — First US Deliveries
Founders Series and early US reservation holders receive vehicles first. This is standard Tesla rollout: US first.
3
Q1–Q2 2027 — European Deliveries
EU type-approval (Euro NCAP, EU homologation) required before European deliveries. Estimated timeline: 6–12 months after US launch.

The Roadster will require EU vehicle type-approval before it can be sold in Europe. Given Tesla's experience with this process (Model S, X, 3, Y all went through it), this is a known process — but it takes 6–12 months post-US production start.

Existing Tesla Roadster Reservations in Europe

Thousands of European buyers placed $50,000 reservations for the Roadster in 2017–2018. If you're one of them, here's what to know:

  • Tesla has kept reservation deposits — your €/$50,000 (or €250,000 Founders) has been held since 2017
  • After the April 1 reveal, Tesla is expected to send order invitations to reservation holders first — in order of reservation date
  • EU reservation holders may receive invitations in the Q1–Q2 2027 window, ahead of general European sales
  • If you placed a reservation and no longer want the vehicle, Tesla's policy is to refund deposits upon request
