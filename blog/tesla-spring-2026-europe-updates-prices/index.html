Spring is traditionally one of the strongest seasons for car buying in Europe — and 2026 is no exception. If you've been watching Tesla prices, following the software updates, or simply wondering whether now is the right moment to pull the trigger, this guide is for you.

Tesla Model Y: Still the King of European EVs

The Tesla Model Y remains Europe's best-selling electric vehicle, and spring 2026 brings fresh reasons to consider one. What's different in the refreshed Model Y: redesigned front and rear fascia, upgraded interior with quieter cabin and ambient lighting, longer range (LR AWD now exceeds 600 km WLTP), and full access to FSD (Supervised) via subscription in most EU markets.

Current European pricing (indicative, VAT included): Model Y RWD from €43,990 · Model Y LR AWD from €52,990 · Model Y Performance from €59,990. Switzerland tends to be CHF 1,000–3,000 higher.

Tesla Model 3 Highland: The Sleeper Hit

The refreshed Model 3 Highland offers a premium interior that rivals German luxury sedans, RWD or LR AWD configurations, updated software, and significantly improved NVH. Current pricing: Model 3 RWD from €39,990 · LR AWD from €50,990 · Performance from €58,990.

What's New in Software: FSD in Europe

Tesla began rolling out FSD (Supervised) v13 to European vehicles in late 2025. By spring 2026 most newer Model 3 and Model Y vehicles in Western Europe have access. It includes automatic lane changes, Navigate on Autopilot, Auto-park, Smart Summon, and city streets FSD in approved areas. It remains Level 2 ADAS — driver must stay attentive. Available as ~€99–129/month subscription.

Tesla Charging in Spring 2026
