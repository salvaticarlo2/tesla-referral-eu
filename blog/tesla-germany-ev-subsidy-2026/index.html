What Changed: Germany's New EV Subsidy Program

Germany's previous EV incentive — the Umweltbonus — was abruptly discontinued in December 2023 when the Federal Constitutional Court struck down the government's reallocation of pandemic emergency funds. This left Germany as one of the few major European markets with no purchase incentive for electric vehicles.

In early 2026, the German government introduced a replacement program with a different design philosophy. Instead of a flat subsidy for all buyers (as the Umweltbonus was), the new program is socially targeted — it offers a base subsidy to all eligible buyers and additional bonuses for lower-income households.

Key differences from the old Umweltbonus:

  • Income cap: There is now a household income limit (€80,000 taxable). The old program had no income restriction.
  • Income-based tiers: Lower-income households get a higher subsidy (up to €8,000 total).
  • Larger budget: Approximately €3 billion, expected to cover ~800,000 vehicles through 2029.
  • Retroactive: Applies from January 1, 2026 — the old program was not retroactive.
  • BEV only: Plug-in hybrids (PHEVs) are excluded. Only battery-electric vehicles qualify.

Who Is Eligible? (Income Limits & Requirements)

The new subsidy is more restrictive than the old Umweltbonus. Here are the eligibility requirements:

1

Income Cap: €80,000 Taxable Household Income

Your household's zu versteuerndes Einkommen (taxable income) must be below €80,000 per year. This is the figure on your tax return (Steuerbescheid), not your gross salary. The cap increases by €5,000 per child, up to a maximum of €90,000 for families.

2

Vehicle: Battery-Electric Only (BEV)

Only fully electric vehicles qualify. Plug-in hybrids, fuel cell vehicles, and range-extended EVs are excluded. All current Tesla models (Model 3, Model Y) are battery-electric and qualify.

3

New Vehicle, Registered in Germany

The vehicle must be a new purchase (not used) and registered in Germany. Import purchases from other EU countries may qualify if the vehicle is first registered in Germany — but buying a Tesla through the German Tesla website is the simplest path.

4

Private Buyers Only

The subsidy targets private individuals, not businesses. Company car buyers have separate benefits (see Dienstwagen section below). However, self-employed individuals (Selbstständige) buying privately may be eligible — check with your tax advisor.

Company Car Buyers (Dienstwagen)

The new subsidy targets private buyers, but company car users in Germany already have a powerful incentive: the 0.25% Dienstwagenbesteuerung rule.

If your employer provides a Tesla as a company car (Dienstwagen) with a list price under €70,000, you're taxed on only 0.25% of the list price per month as a benefit-in-kind — compared to 1% for combustion-engine vehicles. For a Model Y RWD at €44,990, that's just €112.48/month in additional taxable income.

Company car buyers who also have private taxable income below the cap may still qualify for the subsidy on a second, privately purchased EV. Consult your Steuerberater for your specific situation.

Has Germany brought back the EV subsidy in 2026?

Yes. After discontinuing the Umweltbonus in December 2023, Germany launched a new EV incentive program in 2026. The new program provides up to €6,000 (base) or €8,000 (with income-based bonuses) for new battery-electric vehicle purchases. It is retroactive to January 1, 2026, and is backed by approximately €3 billion in federal funding.
