Austrian EV Incentives in 2026

Austria offers a combination of federal and regional incentives that can meaningfully reduce the cost of a Tesla:

Federal Subsidy (Umweltbonus)

Austria's federal EV incentive program has evolved over the years. As of 2026, confirm current eligibility and amounts at umweltförderung.at, as subsidy budgets are renewed annually.

VAT Exemption for Company Cars

For business buyers, electric vehicles can qualify for a full VAT (20%) deduction on the purchase price — a significant advantage over combustion vehicles, which are limited. An Austrian GmbH or sole trader purchasing a Model Y Long Range could recover over €9,000 in VAT.

Additionally, EVs registered as company cars face 0% benefit-in-kind (Sachbezug) tax, compared to up to 2% for combustion vehicles. For a €55,000 Model Y, this saves employees roughly €1,100/month in personal income tax — a major driver of corporate fleet adoption.

Regional Programs

Several Austrian states (Bundesländer) offer additional incentives:

  • Vienna: e-Mobility subsidies for both private buyers and businesses
  • Upper Austria: Grants for private charging installation
  • Styria and Salzburg: Periodic subsidy programs — check regional energy agencies

NoVA Exemption

Austria's vehicle registration tax (Normverbrauchsabgabe / NoVA) does not apply to fully electric vehicles. On a mid-range Tesla, this saves €2,000–€5,000 compared to a combustion equivalent.

Charging in Austria

Tesla Supercharger Network

Austria has excellent Supercharger coverage as of 2026:

  • Key hubs: Vienna (multiple locations), Salzburg, Linz, Innsbruck, Graz, Klagenfurt
  • Motorway coverage: All major Austrian motorways (A1, A2, A9, A10, A12) have Supercharger stations
  • Cross-border charging: Superchargers near borders with Germany (Salzburg, Bregenz), Switzerland (Feldkirch), and Italy (Villach) make international travel seamless

V3 Superchargers (up to 250 kW) are the standard at most Austrian locations. A 15-minute charge adds ~200 km of range to a Model Y Long Range.

Home Charging

Most Austrian Tesla owners charge overnight at home using:

  • Tesla Wall Connector: ~€500–€700 installed; adds up to 44 km/h of range
  • Type 2 portable cable: included with new Teslas; works with standard Austrian household (Schuko) sockets and public charge points

Electricity prices in Austria vary, but at typical residential rates (~€0.25–€0.35/kWh in 2026), a full Model Y charge costs roughly €14–€20 — a fraction of fuel costs.

Public Charging

The Austrian public charging network has expanded significantly. Key providers include IONITY (high-power, 350 kW), SMATRICS EnBW, Wien Energie, and Energie AG. Tesla's CCS-compatible charging means access to the full public network.
