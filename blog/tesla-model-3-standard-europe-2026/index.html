Range & Specifications

The Model 3 Standard Range Europe spec sheet for 2026:

  • WLTP Range: 534km (332 miles)
  • Drivetrain: Rear-Wheel Drive (RWD)
  • Motor: Single permanent magnet motor
  • 0–100 km/h: ~6.1 seconds
  • Top Speed: 201 km/h
  • Charging (DC fast): up to 170kW peak (Supercharger V3)
  • Onboard charger (AC): 11kW
  • Battery: LFP chemistry (no degradation concerns for daily charging to 100%)

The LFP (lithium iron phosphate) battery is a key advantage: you can charge to 100% daily without long-term degradation risk — unlike NMC batteries used in some competitors. Tesla recommends LFP owners charge to 100% regularly.

At 534km WLTP, you're looking at approximately 420–460km of real-world range in mixed conditions (highway + urban). In Norway or Sweden in winter, expect real-world range to drop to 350–400km.

Model 3 Standard Range vs Long Range: Which to Buy?

The Long Range (LR) variant is available from ~€47,990 in Germany and offers:

  • 629km WLTP range (vs 534km Standard)
  • Dual motor AWD (vs single motor RWD)
  • 0–100 in 4.4 seconds (vs 6.1 seconds)

Buy the Standard Range if:

  • Your daily commute is under 150km round trip
  • You have reliable home or workplace charging
  • The ~€9,000 price difference matters to you
  • You live in a mild climate (Southern/Central Europe)

Buy the Long Range if:

  • You regularly drive 400km+ without stopping
  • You drive in cold climates (Nordic countries) where battery range drops significantly
  • You need AWD for winter driving
  • You want the fastest performance

For the majority of European city drivers, the Standard Range covers daily needs with room to spare. The Long Range is a worthy upgrade for road-trip enthusiasts and Nordic buyers.
