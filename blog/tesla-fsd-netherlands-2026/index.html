The Netherlands just made history. On March 4, 2026, the Dutch vehicle authority RDW granted Tesla Extended Type Approval for FSD Supervised — making the Netherlands the first EU country to officially approve Tesla's advanced driver assistance system for public road use.

Full UN-R-171 type approval is confirmed for April 10, 2026. If you own a Tesla in the Netherlands — or you're about to buy one — here's exactly what this means for you.

The RDW Approval — What Happened

On February 12, 2026, Tesla Europe announced FSD Supervised activation via the @teslaeurope channel. The RDW then granted Extended Type Approval on March 4, following a review process that included Tesla publicly demonstrating FSD on Dutch city roads.

That demonstration was significant. Tesla's FSD Supervised navigated busy Dutch city centre traffic — handling roundabouts (a staple of Dutch road design), cyclist-heavy intersections, tram crossings, and pedestrian zones. For Dutch drivers accustomed to sharing road space with millions of cyclists, this was the real test — and FSD passed.

The Extended Type Approval allows Tesla to operate FSD Supervised in the Netherlands under specific conditions monitored by the RDW. Early access invites have already been sent to Dutch Tesla owners, and the first real-world drives are happening now.

April 10: Full UN-R-171 Type Approval

The next — and bigger — milestone is April 10, 2026. This is when the RDW is expected to grant full UN Regulation No. 171 type approval for FSD Supervised. UN-R-171 is the formal EU-wide standard for automated lane-keeping systems.

Why does this matter? Because UN-R-171 approval in one EU member state triggers mutual recognition across the entire EU. Once the Netherlands grants it, countries like Germany, France, and Italy can recognise the approval without starting their own review from zero. The Netherlands is opening the door for all of Europe.

The original target date was March 20, 2026, but the RDW pushed it to April 10 to complete final validation steps. This is a two-day countdown now.

What FSD Supervised Does on Dutch Roads

Tesla FSD Supervised — officially rebranded to Navigate on Autosteer in Europe as of update 2026.2.9 — handles driving across highways and city streets. On Dutch roads specifically, this means:

  • Roundabouts: The Netherlands has over 4,000 roundabouts. FSD navigates them, yielding to traffic already in the circle and handling multi-lane exits
  • Cyclist awareness: Dutch roads have dedicated fietspaden (bike lanes) alongside nearly every road. FSD's vision system detects cyclists and gives appropriate space
  • Tram crossings: In Amsterdam, Rotterdam, and Den Haag, tram lines intersect with road traffic. FSD recognises tram signals and yields accordingly
  • Traffic lights and stop signs: Full recognition and response, including the Dutch-style traffic lights with separate bicycle signals
  • Highway driving: Lane changes, merging, and navigation on the A-roads and motorways
  • Urban navigation: Complex city centre driving including narrow streets, parking manoeuvres, and pedestrian zones

The driver must remain attentive at all times — FSD is supervised, not autonomous. Hands must be ready to take the wheel, and eyes should stay on the road.

FSD Pricing in the Netherlands

FSD Supervised is a paid upgrade, separate from the basic Autopilot included with every Tesla. Here's what Dutch buyers can expect:

  • One-time purchase: Starting from approximately €3,000 in the Netherlands — lower than the US price of $8,000
  • Monthly subscription: Expected after full type approval on April 10. US pricing is $99–$199/month; expect a similar range in euros
  • EAP upgrade: If you already have Enhanced Autopilot, Tesla is expected to offer a discounted path to FSD

FSD is tied to the vehicle. If you sell your Tesla, the next owner keeps FSD. If you subscribe monthly, you can cancel at any time.

How to Activate FSD in the Netherlands

If you're a Dutch Tesla owner who's received an early access invite, here's what to do:

  1. Check the Tesla app — look for an FSD notification or software update prompt
  2. Connect to WiFi — FSD updates are large and require a stable WiFi connection (not mobile data)
  3. Purchase or subscribe — if you haven't already bought FSD, you can purchase it through the Tesla app under Upgrades, or wait for the subscription option after April 10
  4. Install the update — once downloaded, the update installs during a scheduled time (usually overnight if plugged in)
  5. First drive — FSD Supervised activates automatically. On your first use, Tesla shows a brief in-car tutorial explaining what FSD can and can't do

If you haven't received an invite yet, don't worry. Tesla is rolling out invites in waves. All eligible Dutch vehicles will have access once full type approval is granted on April 10.

What Comes After the Netherlands?

The Netherlands is the first domino. After April 10 UN-R-171 approval, other EU countries can follow through mutual recognition:

  • Germany, France, Norway: Expected summer 2026
  • UK: Separate approval (non-EU) but testing already approved, summer 2026 expected
  • Italy, Spain, Switzerland: Late 2026

FAQ — Tesla FSD Netherlands

Is Tesla FSD approved in the Netherlands?

Yes. The RDW granted Extended Type Approval on March 4, 2026. Full UN-R-171 type approval is confirmed for April 10, 2026. The Netherlands is the first EU country with FSD Supervised approval.

Do I need to pay extra for FSD on my Dutch Tesla?
